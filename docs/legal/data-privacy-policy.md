## 1. Introduction and Legal Framework

This Privacy Policy describes how Rethink Carbon ("Company," "Processor," "we," "our," or "us"), a company incorporated under the laws of Pakistan, processes Personal Data through its cloud-based carbon accounting SaaS platform ("Platform").

This Policy is drafted in alignment with:

- Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (General Data Protection Regulation - "GDPR")
- GDPR Articles 5, 6, 24, 28, 32, 33, 44-49 and related provisions
- International SaaS security best practices

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities (GDPR Articles 4, 24, 28)

### 2.1 Customer as Controller

Where Customer uploads or inputs data, Customer acts as Data Controller (Art. 4(7)).

### 2.2 Rethink Carbon as Processor

Rethink Carbon acts as Data Processor (Art. 4(8)) and processes Personal Data solely on documented instructions of the Controller pursuant to Article 28.

### 2.3 Independent Controller Data

For account management, billing, and security monitoring, Rethink Carbon acts as an independent Controller.

## 3. Principles of Processing (Article 5)

In accordance with Article 5 GDPR, the Company processes Personal Data in strict adherence to the following core principles:

### 3.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

Personal Data is processed only where a valid lawful basis under Article 6 (and, where applicable, Article 9) exists. Processing activities are conducted in a manner that is fair, proportionate, and transparent to data subjects. The Company ensures that Controllers are provided with clear information regarding processing activities to enable compliance with Articles 12-14 GDPR.

### 3.2 Purpose Limitation

Personal Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes related to carbon accounting, emissions analytics, financial portfolio analysis, platform administration, security monitoring, and contractual service delivery. Data is not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes unless otherwise permitted by applicable law.

### 3.3 Data Minimization

The Company processes only Personal Data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed. System configurations, data schemas, and collection interfaces are designed to avoid excessive data intake.

### 3.4 Accuracy

Reasonable steps are taken to ensure Personal Data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date. The Company provides mechanisms enabling Controllers to update or correct data and relies on Controllers to ensure the accuracy of data they provide.

### 3.5 Storage Limitation

Personal Data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected and in accordance with Section 6 (Data Retention). Upon expiration of the retention period, data is securely deleted or irreversibly anonymized, subject to legal retention requirements.

### 3.6 Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)

Personal Data is processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. Technical and organizational measures implemented pursuant to Article 32 include encryption, access controls, monitoring, and secure hosting infrastructure.

### 3.7 Accountability

The Company is responsible for, and able to demonstrate compliance with these principles. This includes maintaining documented policies, records of processing activities, internal governance controls, staff training, and audit mechanisms consistent with Articles 24 and 30 GDPR.

## 5. Lawful Bases for Processing (Article 6)

The Company processes Personal Data strictly in accordance with the lawful bases set out in Article 6 GDPR and, where applicable, Article 9 GDPR (special categories of data). The applicable lawful basis depends on the context in which the data is processed and the role of the Company (Controller or Processor).

### 5.1 Article 6(1)(b) - Contractual Necessity

Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the Customer is a party, including provision of access to the Platform, execution of emissions analytics, generation of reports, account administration, technical support, billing, and performance of obligations under applicable Order Forms and service agreements. Where Personal Data is required to deliver configured functionalities of the Platform, such processing is deemed contractually necessary.

### 5.2 Article 6(1)(f) - Legitimate Interests

Processing is necessary for the purposes of legitimate interests pursued by the Company or the Customer, provided such interests are not overridden by the fundamental rights and freedoms of data subjects. Legitimate interests may include: (i) ensuring platform security and system integrity; (ii) preventing fraud, misuse, or unauthorized access; (iii) improving product performance and user experience; (iv) conducting internal analytics and service optimization; (v) defending legal claims; and (vi) maintaining audit trails and compliance documentation. Where reliance is placed on legitimate interests, the Company performs balancing assessments to ensure proportionality.

### 5.3 Article 6(1)(c) - Legal Obligation

Processing may be necessary for compliance with legal or regulatory obligations applicable to the Company or the Customer, including obligations relating to financial regulation, environmental reporting, anti-money laundering frameworks, audit requirements, sanctions compliance, court orders, or supervisory authority requests.

### 5.4 Article 6(1)(a) - Consent

Where required by applicable law, processing is based on the data subject's explicit, informed, and freely given consent. This may apply where specific categories of sensitive or special category data are processed, or where required under local data protection legislation. Data subjects retain the right to withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

### 5.5 Article 6(1)(d) - Vital Interests (Where Applicable)

In limited and exceptional circumstances, processing may be necessary to protect the vital interests of a data subject or another natural person, where such processing is strictly required.

### 5.6 Article 6(1)(e) - Public Interest (Where Applicable)

Where processing is carried out in the context of tasks performed in the public interest or under official authority vested in a Controller, such processing shall rely on the lawful basis established by the relevant Controller.

Where the Company acts as a Processor, it processes Personal Data solely on documented instructions of the Controller pursuant to Article 28 GDPR, and the Controller remains responsible for determining and documenting the appropriate lawful basis under Article 6.

## 7. Security of Processing (Article 32)

In accordance with Article 32 GDPR, the Company implements appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, implementation costs, the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing, and the likelihood and severity of risks to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.

Such measures include, without limitation:

### 7.1 Encryption and Data Protection Controls

- Encryption of Personal Data in transit using industry-standard TLS protocols
- Encryption of Personal Data at rest using strong cryptographic standards (e.g., AES-256 or equivalent)
- Secure key management practices and restricted cryptographic key access
- Logical data segregation between customers within shared infrastructure environments

### 7.2 Access Control and Identity Management

- Role-based access control (RBAC) aligned with least-privilege principles
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for administrative and privileged access
- Strong password policies and credential lifecycle management
- Periodic access reviews and revocation procedures upon role change or termination

### 7.3 Network and Infrastructure Security

- Deployment within secure AWS cloud environments with network isolation mechanisms
- Firewalls, intrusion detection and prevention systems, and traffic monitoring controls
- Environment segregation between production, staging, and development systems
- Secure configuration management and hardened system baselines

### 7.4 Monitoring, Logging, and Incident Detection

- Continuous monitoring of system activity and anomalous behavior
- Centralized logging of access and system events
- Retention of audit trails sufficient to support forensic analysis and regulatory compliance
- Documented incident response procedures and escalation frameworks

### 7.5 Vulnerability Management and Testing

- Regular vulnerability scanning and security assessments
- Periodic penetration testing conducted internally or by qualified third parties
- Timely remediation of identified security weaknesses based on risk severity
- Secure software development lifecycle (SDLC) practices including code review and testing

### 7.6 Organizational and Governance Measures

- Written information security and data protection policies
- Confidentiality agreements binding employees and contractors
- Regular security awareness and data protection training
- Vendor risk assessments prior to onboarding subprocessors
- Periodic internal compliance reviews and risk assessments

The Company periodically reviews and updates its technical and organizational measures to reflect evolving threats, regulatory developments, and industry best practices.

## 9. International Transfers (Articles 44-49)

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA), the Company implements transfer mechanisms in compliance with GDPR Chapter V (Articles 44-49), as follows:

(a) **Standard Contractual Clauses ("SCCs")** - Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914. Where transfers occur from the EEA to a third country without an adequacy decision under Article 45, the Company enters into the applicable modular SCCs (Controller-to-Processor or Processor-to-Processor, as relevant). These clauses are incorporated into data processing agreements and impose enforceable data protection obligations on the data importer.

(b) **Transfer Impact Assessments ("TIAs")**. Prior to relying on SCCs, the Company conducts a documented assessment of the legal and regulatory environment of the destination jurisdiction to evaluate whether additional technical, contractual, or organizational safeguards are required to ensure an essentially equivalent level of protection as required by Article 46.

(c) **Supplementary Safeguards**. Where necessary, additional measures may include enhanced encryption, pseudonymization, strict access controls, contractual audit rights, and transparency obligations.

(d) **Equivalent Safeguards**. Where applicable, transfers may rely on an adequacy decision under Article 45, Binding Corporate Rules (Article 47), or other lawful derogations under Article 49.

(e) **Processor-to-Subprocessor Agreements**. Where the Company engages subprocessors located outside the EEA, written agreements incorporating Article 28 requirements and SCC modules are executed to ensure onward transfer protections equivalent to those imposed on the Company.

## 11. Data Subject Rights (Articles 12-23)

In accordance with Articles 12-23 GDPR, data subjects whose Personal Data is processed through the Platform are entitled to exercise the following rights, subject to applicable legal limitations and exemptions:

### 11.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

Data subjects have the right to obtain confirmation as to whether their Personal Data is being processed and, where that is the case, access to the Personal Data and supplementary information including the purposes of processing, categories of data concerned, recipients or categories of recipients, retention periods, and safeguards for international transfers.

### 11.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

Data subjects have the right to obtain without undue delay the rectification of inaccurate Personal Data and the completion of incomplete Personal Data, taking into account the purposes of the processing.

### 11.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Article 17)

Data subjects may request the deletion of Personal Data where one of the grounds under Article 17 applies, including where data is no longer necessary for the purposes collected, consent has been withdrawn, or processing is unlawful. This right is subject to limitations, including where retention is required for compliance with legal obligations or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

### 11.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

Data subjects may request restriction of processing in circumstances including disputes regarding data accuracy, unlawful processing where erasure is opposed, or where the data subject requires the data for legal claims.

### 11.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

Where processing is based on consent or contractual necessity and carried out by automated means, data subjects have the right to receive their Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and, where technically feasible, to transmit that data to another controller.

### 11.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

Data subjects have the right to object, on grounds relating to their particular situation, to processing based on Article 6(1)(e) or (f), including profiling. Where such objection is valid, processing shall cease unless compelling legitimate grounds override the interests, rights, and freedoms of the data subject.

### 11.7 Rights Relating to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

Where applicable, data subjects have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects or similarly significant effects, except where permitted under Article 22(2) GDPR.

### 11.8 Exercise of Rights

Where the Company acts as a Processor, it shall promptly notify the relevant Controller of any request received directly from a data subject and shall not respond to such request except on documented instructions from the Controller, unless legally required to do so. The Company shall provide reasonable assistance to enable the Controller to fulfill its obligations under Articles 12-23 GDPR.

## 12. Personal Data Breach Notification (Articles 33-34)

### 12.1 Definition of Personal Data Breach

For purposes of this Agreement, a "Personal Data Breach" shall have the meaning set forth in Article 4(12) GDPR and includes any breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

### 12.2 Notification to Controller

In accordance with Article 33(2) GDPR, the Company shall notify the Controller without undue delay and, where feasible, within seventy-two (72) hours after becoming aware of a confirmed Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Data. Where notification within seventy-two (72) hours is not feasible, the Company shall provide the reasons for delay and deliver the required information in phases as it becomes available.

### 12.3 Content of Notification

Such notification shall include, to the extent known at the time:

(a) a description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including categories and approximate number of data subjects and data records concerned;  
(b) the name and contact details of the data protection contact point;  
(c) a description of the likely consequences of the breach; and  
(d) a description of the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address and mitigate the breach.

### 12.4 Assistance to Controller

The Company shall provide reasonable cooperation and assistance to enable the Controller to comply with its obligations under Articles 33 and 34 GDPR, including assistance with regulatory notifications and, where required, communication to affected data subjects.

### 12.5 Containment and Remediation

The Company shall promptly take appropriate technical and organizational measures to contain, investigate, remediate, and mitigate the effects of the Personal Data Breach and shall document all relevant facts, impacts, and corrective actions taken in accordance with Article 33(5) GDPR.

### 12.6 No Admission of Liability

Notification of a Personal Data Breach under this Section shall not be construed as an admission of fault or liability by the Company.
